Abstract

Food safety remains a critical global imperative, as evidenced by the United Nations prioritizing 'Zero Hunger' and food security as its second Sustainable Development Goal . While regions such as Hong Kong maintain rigorous food safety standards and comprehensive safety protocols, regulatory oversights often leave populations vulnerable to enteric pathogens. Among these, Campylobacter jejuni (C. jejuni) represents a pervasive threat; the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) estimates that it is the leading cause of Campylobacteriosis, with approximately 1.5 million infections annually in the United States alone, contributing to significant socio-economic and healthcare burdens. Current diagnostic 'gold standards' rely on culture-based methods that take several days to yield results, a time-frame incompatible with the rapid turnover of perishable food supply chains. To bridge this gap, we propose the development of a rapid, paper-based diagnostic tool for the detection of C. jejuni within diverse food matrices. This proposal elucidates the pathogenic mechanisms and systemic health impacts of C. jejuni, details our novel detection methodology and experimental protocols, and provides a comprehensive budget and strategic work schedule.
